Nagpur—nice, you’re asking about a city that’s way more interesting than people give it credit for.

###### 📍 The basics

Nagpur is a major city in the state of Maharashtra, right in the *geographical center of India*. In fact, there’s a literal marker called
👉 Zero Mile Stone that marks this central point.

---

### 🍊 Why it’s called the “Orange City”

Nagpur is famous for its juicy oranges—seriously, they’re kind of a big deal. The region’s climate makes them extra sweet, and orange trading is a huge part of the local economy.

---

### 🏛️ Culture & history

* It has deep historical roots tied to the Maratha Empire.
* It’s also important for modern Indian history—
  👉 B. R. Ambedkar converted to Buddhism here in 1956 along with thousands of followers.
  👉 That event is commemorated at
  Deekshabhoomi, one of the largest Buddhist monuments in the world.

---

### 🌳 Places to check out

* Futala Lake – great for evening vibes and street food
* Ambazari Lake and Garden – chill nature spot
* Sitabuldi Fort – a historic hilltop fort
* Maharajbagh Zoo – small but popular local zoo

---

### 🚆 Connectivity & importance

Nagpur is a major transportation hub—roads, railways, and even cargo routes intersect here. There’s also a big logistics project called MIHAN (Multi-modal International Cargo Hub), making it important for trade and development.

---

### 🌡️ Weather reality check

Not gonna sugarcoat it—Nagpur gets *hot*. Summers can go above 45°C 😅
Winters though? Pretty pleasant.

---

### 🍽️ Food vibe

* Famous for spicy *Saoji cuisine* (not for the faint-hearted 🔥)
* Street food is big—chaat, samosas, and late-night snacks around lakes
